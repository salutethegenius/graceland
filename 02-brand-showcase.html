South Bahamia · Freeport · Grand Bahama
01 / FOUNDATION

Quiet luxury,
rooted in place.

Graceland is not a hotel and not an Airbnb. It sits in the deliberate category of boutique vacation clubs — properties that combine the comfort of a private home with the service of hospitality. The brand carries two audiences without leaning toward either: the curated traveler and the hospitality investor.

Pillar 01
Curated

Nothing is generic. Every suite is named, every experience is selected.

Pillar 02
Bahamian

Rooted in Grand Bahama and the wider archipelago. The names anchor the place.

Pillar 03
Boutique

Small by design. Personal by default. The opposite of resort-scale.

Pillar 04
Earned

Investor-credible. Substance over claims. Quiet competence.

02 / PALETTE

Navy dominates.
Gold is rare.

Deep navy is the brand's primary surface. Gold is reserved for the monogram, primary calls-to-action, and key wordmark moments. Bone takes over when the brand needs to feel open or hospitable — booking flows, investor packets, in-suite collateral.

05 / VOICE

Composed.
Specific. Hospitable.

The voice never breathless, never hard-selling. It names places, names suites, names experiences. It avoids "amazing," "stunning," "unforgettable." It is the voice of a good host, not a brochure.

— Don't write

"Welcome to The Amelia Suite, an absolutely stunning 2-bedroom oasis where luxury meets paradise! Book now for an unforgettable experience!"

— Do write

"The Amelia Suite. Two bedrooms, two baths, and a kitchen built for slow mornings. Twenty minutes from the airport."

06 / NAMING

A system that scales.

Graceland is the parent. Suites take place names. Amenities take function names. Service tiers take the Graceland prefix. The architecture lets the brand grow without fragmenting.
