A New Standard

From boutique hospitality to a resilience platform.

Graceland has evolved into a climate-smart, digitally enabled tourism and accommodation ecosystem with measurable economic and social outcomes.

Graceland still delivers a high-quality guest stay — but the model now targets a broader mission: tourism competitiveness, workforce lodging capacity, and climate resilience in Grand Bahama.

Phase 1 is delivered and operational. The next phase is structured to expand accommodations, strengthen event and hospitality throughput, and improve long-term asset resilience.

For guests, it remains a private South Bahamia destination. For public and private partners, it is a practical blueprint for resilient, women-led tourism development in the Caribbean.
